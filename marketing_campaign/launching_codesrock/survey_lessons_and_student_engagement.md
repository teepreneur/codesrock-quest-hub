## Part 3: Tactical Programming (Logic Cards & Rockbot)

### 4. Logic Card Handling & "Map" Phase
*How well did students understand the translation of physical Logic Cards (Forward, Turn, Loop) into Rockbot movements?*
- [ ] 5 – Instantly mapped cards to Mat coordinates
- [ ] 4 – Good understanding; required minimal assistance
- [ ] 3 – Neutral / Required moderate teacher intervention
- [ ] 2 – Struggled to understand the card order (e.g., placing cards right-to-left)
- [ ] 1 – Could not grasp how the cards programmed the robot

### 5. Rockbot Navigation & Group Cooperation
*We recommend grouping students (4 per Rockbot). How did students handle collaboration and turn-taking during Mat navigation?*
- [ ] 5 – Excellent teamwork; students naturally assigned roles (Card Placer, Mat Checker, Robot Trigger)
- [ ] 4 – Worked together well, with occasional conflicts
- [ ] 3 – Neutral / Had to micro-manage group turn-taking
- [ ] 2 – Poor cooperation; 1 or 2 students dominated the robot while others watched
- [ ] 1 – Chaotic; groups were too large/unruly

### 6. Debugging & The "Error-Positive" Culture
*How did your students react when the Rockbot did not reach the target coordinate (i.e. when their code had a bug)?*
- [ ] 5 – Excitement; they immediately began debugging the cards collaboratively (the "bug hunting" mindset)
- [ ] 4 – Frustrated initially, but successfully resolved the bug
- [ ] 3 – Needed teacher prompts to fix the path
- [ ] 2 – Gave up when the code failed
- [ ] 1 – Disappointed / Stopped playing
